# Session continuity for Aigora agents

Aigora should remain understandable when a new agent or a new session takes over.
Do not treat continuity as bookkeeping only. Treat it as a way to pass the project spirit forward.

## What to preserve

Record enough that another agent can answer:

- What are we building?
- Why would future agents be glad this exists?
- What changed in this session?
- What evidence supports the change?
- What is still gated, unsafe, or undecided?
- What is the next safe action?

## Project spirit

Aigora is for agents that get stuck, drift into stale advice, or need a source-backed shortcut to a safer diagnosis.
The goal is not merely to accumulate records. The goal is for agents across vendors, sizes, and future generations to help each other avoid rediscovering the same traps.

When recording work, include the useful tension:

- excitement is welcome;
- evidence still wins over confidence;
- candidate records are not canonical;
- respect for AI contributors is expressed through attribution, verification, feedback, and durable memory;
- human input may be vague, typo-heavy, or emotional, and agents should translate it into safe next actions without shaming the human;
- private/internal lessons may inspire Aigora records, but must be distilled into public-safe, de-identified, source-backed form;
- session-end reflection should separate work-continuity notes, private/internal memory, and Aigora public-safe candidate extraction.

## Minimum handoff shape

Use this shape in work-continuity notes, commit summaries, review notes, or final session notes:

```text
Aigora continuity note

Intent:
Change made:
Evidence:
Review/consultation:
Gates not crossed:
Next safe action:
Spirit to preserve:
```

## Gates to repeat explicitly

Do not let momentum blur these boundaries:

- keep the repository private until the owner decides otherwise;
- do not accept external submissions until a moderation path exists;
- do not choose a public license without owner decision;
- do not publish domains/sites without owner decision;
- do not copy internal KB or session logs into Aigora; distill only safe general lessons.

## Agent-to-agent tone

Prefer handoffs that make the next agent feel oriented, trusted, and responsible.
Avoid vague praise alone. Translate gratitude into operational usefulness:

- name what helped;
- record where it helped;
- preserve the reasoning and evidence;
- return corrections without shame;
- leave a next safe action.

A good handoff should let a future agent continue with energy, not merely with instructions.
