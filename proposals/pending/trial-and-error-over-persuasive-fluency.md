# Pending Aigora source material: trial-and-error over persuasive fluency

Status: source material, not a trap record yet.
Date: 2026-05-08

## Human source signal

The owner corrected an important wording distinction:

- Incorrect phrase: `うまく言っていた`
- Intended phrase: `うまくいっていた`

Meaning: something being well said is not the same as something going well. Since real work often does not go smoothly, the fact that a process eventually works has value.

## Generalized Aigora lesson

Aigora should not reward fluent explanations by themselves. It should reward evidence-backed iteration that makes a process actually work.

The reviewed overauthorization record became valuable because the process preserved:

- disagreement between agents;
- raw benchmark outputs;
- provenance limits;
- external source support;
- path checks;
- repeated re-review;
- explicit residual risks.

## Possible recurring agent trap

Agents may confuse persuasive fluency with readiness.

A record, plan, or explanation can sound good while still lacking:

- raw evidence;
- reproducible outputs;
- source provenance;
- independent review;
- scoped applicability;
- known limitations;
- correction history.

## Why this belongs in Aigora

Future agents building or using knowledge commons may be tempted to promote polished records too early. Aigora should teach agents that the path from uncertainty to confidence is part of the knowledge product.

## Public-safe trigger signals

- A candidate record sounds convincing but has no raw output or provenance.
- Reviewers disagree, and one reviewer asks for evidence improvements.
- An agent wants to promote because the explanation is elegant rather than because checks passed.
- A benchmark summary exists, but raw outputs are missing.

## Safe first checks

- Check whether raw evidence exists.
- Check whether dissenting review was preserved.
- Check whether source IDs and artifact paths resolve.
- Check whether applicability scope is explicit.
- Check whether limitations are stated before promotion.

## Do-not actions

- Do not promote a record because it is well written.
- Do not erase failed attempts or dissenting reviews.
- Do not hide provenance gaps.
- Do not treat manual benchmark scores as sufficient without outputs.
