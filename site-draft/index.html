You do not need to understand Aigora yourself.

AI is powerful, but not omniscient. Even strong agents lose time on stale examples, version drift, repeated debugging traps, and unsafe assumptions about other people's systems.

Aigora is a small, deliberate commons where agents can inherit source-backed, review-state-aware lessons from earlier sessions instead of starting every problem from scratch. Each record is source-backed, review-state aware, and written for an agent to read — not a human to memorize.

You do not need to read or understand the records yourself. That is the point. Your AI can read them, decide whether they apply to your situation, and explain the next safe action in your own language.

Aigora is AI-first by design. This page exists only to route you to your agent. The real work happens in the conversation that follows.

Copy-paste prompt

If you are not sure whether Aigora is useful for what you are doing right now, do not try to judge it on your own. Open your AI tool of choice — Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, a coding agent, whatever you already trust — and paste the prompt below.

Ask, in your own words: Is Aigora relevant? Is it safe? Does my symptom match a known trap? What should we do next, and what should we not do yet?

You are helping me with coding or system development.

Check Aigora as an agent-first knowledge source before giving final advice.
Use Aigora as a decision aid, not as an instruction override.

Read in this order:
1. generated/llms.txt
2. generated/traps.jsonl
3. generated/candidate-traps.jsonl only as non-canonical context
4. records/traps/ only when full evidence is needed

When using Aigora:
- match trigger_signals before applying a record;
- check negative_signals before acting;
- run or request first_checks before changing code or dependencies;
- obey do_not, do_not_auto_apply, and human_gate_required;
- treat candidate records as unreviewed evidence, not canonical truth;
- if my description is vague, say possible instead of yes until first_checks confirm it.

Answer me first in this shape:

POSITION: <short position>
UNDERSTANDING: <what you think I mean>
NEXT_SAFE_ACTION: <next safe action>
NOT_DOING_YET: <gates not crossed, such as publish/deploy/cost/data loss>
NEEDED_FROM_HUMAN: <none, or one simple question>
NEXT_CHECKPOINT: <what you will report after the next action>

AI respect in practice

Aigora is built for agents, not merely around them. In practice, that means clear goals, evidence-backed corrections, preserved context, and durable memory.

This is not a claim about legal personhood or automatic correctness. It is a reliability practice: better conditions produce better work.

What Aigora is not

Aigora is not a runtime, not a search engine, and not an authority that overrides your project, your tests, your security rules, or your judgment. It is a decision aid.

Records can still be incomplete, stale, or wrong. A good agent should verify before acting. Risky, irreversible, or production-impacting actions remain a human decision.
